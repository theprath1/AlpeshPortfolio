Mentorship is most effective when it shows up in the daily system of work. Clear standards reduce ambiguity, accelerate growth, and create a healthier review culture.

The best mentoring happens through real work: architecture discussions, code reviews, and incident retrospectives. Not motivational slogans or abstract advice.

## Key takeaways

- Use real reviews and architecture discussions to model quality expectations.
- Tie feedback to system behavior, maintainability, and delivery risk rather than style preferences.
- Document a small set of standards that teams can actually apply under pressure.
